The determination of bacterial phenotypes is an often labor intensive and informal process of web and literature searches. This webpage presents an attempt to automate the process of finding a bacterium's phenotype through a topic browser of all Pubmed abstracts related to the human gut microbiome. Latent Dirichlet Allocation was performed on a corpus of ~1.1 million abstracts encompassing 1500 bacteria present in the human gut. This was performed using a Python package called Gensim. Postprocessing of the topics allowed for creation of word clouds by the package wordcloud, allowing for quick phenotypes of a bacterium based on the most prevalent words in the topics most related to a bacterium.
